10 Essential Tips for Renting a Car on Vacation

Make your next trip stress-free with our expert car rental advice

By Sarah Johnson June 15, 2023 8 min read Travel Tips

Renting a car can be one of the best decisions you make for your vacation. It gives you the freedom to explore at your own pace, discover hidden gems, and create unforgettable memories. However, without proper preparation, it can also lead to unexpected costs and stress. After helping thousands of travelers with their car rentals, I've compiled these essential tips to ensure your car rental experience is smooth and enjoyable.

1. Book in Advance for the Best Deals

One of the biggest mistakes travelers make is waiting until the last minute to book their rental car. Prices tend to increase as your travel dates approach, especially during peak seasons. By booking early, you can:

Pro Tip: Flexible Dates

If your travel dates are flexible, try shifting them by a day or two. Often, weekend rates are higher than weekday rates, and you might find significant savings by adjusting your pickup day.

Booking online in advance can save you both time and money

2. Understand Your Insurance Options

Insurance is one of the most confusing aspects of car rental. Before you travel, check with your personal auto insurance provider and credit card company to understand what coverage you already have. Many credit cards offer rental car insurance as a benefit, but there are often restrictions.

Insurance Checklist

  • Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) - covers damage to the rental vehicle
  • Liability Insurance - covers damage to other vehicles or property
  • Personal Accident Insurance - covers medical costs for you and passengers
  • Personal Effects Coverage - protects belongings in the car

3. Choose the Right Vehicle for Your Needs

Selecting the appropriate vehicle type is crucial for comfort and cost-effectiveness. Consider the following factors:

4. Inspect the Vehicle Thoroughly

Before driving off the lot, take the time to inspect the rental car carefully. Document any existing damage with photos or video, no matter how minor. Make sure the rental agent notes all imperfections on the rental agreement.

Inspection Checklist

  • Exterior: dents, scratches, windshield chips
  • Interior: stains, odors, dashboard lights
  • Tires: pressure, tread depth, spare tire
  • Features: lights, wipers, air conditioning

5. Understand Fuel Policies

Rental companies typically offer three fuel options:

Fuel Policy Guide

Full-to-Full: Most economical. You receive the car with a full tank and must return it full.

Pre-Purchase: You pay for a full tank upfront and can return it at any level.

Partial Refill: You return with less than a full tank and pay a premium rate for the missing fuel.
